# Style Presets

Pick one main style preset. Do not casually mix several styles in one deck.

## government-report

Use for leadership briefings, policy reports, public-sector topics, safety, infrastructure, and formal decision support.

- Feel: steady, credible, restrained, evidence-led
- Layout: clear hierarchy, section bands, map/data panels, decision blocks
- Colors: off-white or pale gray background, deep blue/green accent, one warning color
- Images: real places, maps, infrastructure, wide documentary visuals
- Motion: restrained section transitions, progressive data reveal

## tech-keynote

Use for AI, SaaS, product launches, innovation, engineering, and future-oriented talks.

- Feel: crisp, confident, spacious, high-contrast
- Layout: large titles, metric walls, animated process diagrams, product flows
- Colors: neutral background, black text, electric blue/green accent
- Images: wide product scenes, abstract technology metaphors, interface close-ups
- Motion: reveal sequencing, line drawing, count-up metrics, smooth slide transitions

## magazine-narrative

Use for专题分享, culture, research storytelling, profiles, public communication, and rich editorial subjects.

- Feel: immersive, human, elegant, story-led
- Layout: full-bleed chapter openers, image-text spreads, quote pages, timeline rhythm
- Colors: warm paper or clean editorial neutrals with one strong accent
- Images: landscape editorial photos or generated cinematic scenes with safe text space
- Motion: image pan/zoom, section openers, gentle text reveals

## swiss-infographic

Use for analysis reports, research, datasets, comparisons, technical explainers, and structured decisions.

- Feel: precise, rational, grid-based, information-dense but calm
- Layout: strict grid, tables, charts, matrices, labeled diagrams
- Colors: white/near-white base, black text, one bright accent plus neutral grays
- Images: minimal; use charts, maps, icons, and diagrams first
- Motion: progressive annotation, chart reveal, tabbed comparisons

## education-explainer

Use for classroom, training, onboarding, public education, and concepts that need patient explanation.

- Feel: clear, paced, friendly, not childish
- Layout: one idea per slide, diagrams, step-by-step reveal, recap pages
- Colors: light neutral base, warm accent, accessible contrast
- Images: diagrams, simple scenes, process visuals
- Motion: click-to-reveal steps, toggles, small concept animations

## boardroom-strategy

Use for executives, investment, business strategy, annual review, and budget decisions.

- Feel: premium, concise, decisive
- Layout: headline claim, evidence, implication, decision ask
- Colors: ivory, charcoal, restrained red/gold/blue accent
- Images: few; use high-quality wide hero images and clean charts
- Motion: subtle reveal, no playful effects

## Selection Guidance

- If the topic is public-sector risk or infrastructure, default to `government-report`.
- If the topic is AI/product/technology, default to `tech-keynote`.
- If the source is long-form narrative or culture, default to `magazine-narrative`.
- If evidence and comparison matter most, default to `swiss-infographic`.
- If the audience is learning a concept, default to `education-explainer`.
- If the audience must approve a decision, default to `boardroom-strategy`.
